This is my inargural blog post on my new website! I'm unsure how frequently I'll be making posts, but with the unhealthy state of social media platforms lately, I figured it was a good idea to try and start this up. I'm starting off by chatting a little bit about the first Open World Shutter Jam - a jam centered on the usage of the Open World Shutter software by Jonny Hopkins. Below you'll find a dump of shots I managed to capture during the span on the jam, many of which were unsubmitted. I've had so much fun with this lately that I had to try and limit myself on the number of entries I submitted for the jam...

I had dabbled with the tool a little bit in the past when it was first released. I have been following Jonny's work for a while - his work in the alt-ctrl games scene has been particularly inspirational to much of my own work. When this tool was released, I was knee-deep in Elden Ring, and the games beautifully erie aesthetic, paired the ability to diable the games HUD and use of the in-game telescope, it felt like the perfect place to test the waters. Despite my love of photography as a hobby, I think this may have been the closest I've ever really come to actually playing with any sort of camera-related mode or tools in (or with) a game.
