Lent is still a little new to me. Coming into a Presbyterian church after growing up as non-denominational, there are still a few aspects I haven't gotten used to yet. In previous years, I would give up game development during the lenten season, to try and recenter my focus on my beliefs and my other responsibilities as a husband and father. This year I made that same attempt, but I thought I would try and make the effort to do more Writing about where my interests in games and play fit within my religious beliefs.

At least I can say I made teh attempt, at least. I ended up not so much giving up game development, but I did greatly pare down my social media usage, primarily with Reddit, Twitter, and Bluesky. I thought that maybe giving up those things would help free up some headspace, and for the most part it did. I ended up deleting my bluesky account, Reddit is still blocked on my phone and work computer, and twitter's timeline is completely disabled by adblocker. I don't think I'll be changing those things any time soon.

My attempt at writing ended up falling a bit flat though. And I think in general, it's just not where my focus needed to be in this season of life. That intersection of play and faith is still one I like to read and learn about, and hopefully I'll be able to share more of what I find in that area of life. However, I feel barely scratched the surface in the two blog posts I did manage to write before Easter came and went, and the fact of the matter is that I simply had more important areas in my life and in my role in my family that needed more immediate care and attention.

I suppose that, while giving up as much social media as I did was a great thing for me, it was also more important for me to simply be open and attentive to the other areas in my life and mind that needed more attention, rather than try to artificially add something myself. If anyone read those two blog posts and perhaps had something spark in their own minds, then great! I think for me though, it's just not the right time to sit and have those discussions. hopefully that will come someday soon. In the meantime though, I have still have more work to do that won't make it to my little corner of the internet as a blog post.

And thats ok. Not everything needs to be shared.
