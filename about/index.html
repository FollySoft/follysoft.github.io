About

My name is Jonathan West, I'm a hobbyist game designer, photographer, and on rare ocassion a writer, based in New Mexico. I'm a proud dad of two (soon to be 3!), and work full time as a R&D Software Developer.

This website at one point served entirely as a portfolio of my game and UX design experience, as a sort of showcase for potential employers. However, as I'm not longer job hunting, I though I could take the opportunity to try to make this space a little bit more my own. Right now, it is still somewhat serving as a portfolio, but I hope to let it grow natrually and organically to serve other, more creative explorations in the web-space.

You'll often find me using photos from Wikimedia Commons, Pexels, or Pixabay in my games and other projects, however each photo background found on this site is from my own camera. Most are available on my Pexels page, where they can be freely downloaded for anyone else's own creative pursuits.

I'm inspired by things that are playful, things that subvert your expectations and get you to crack a smile at the results. We live in an age that treats technology as both a luxury and a necessity, as priceless tools that work for us rather than play along with us. To me, the best creative works are the ones that show us otherwise. Some of my favorite pieces of playful technology and digital games include:

  • J.S. Joust by Die Gute Fabrik
  • Alphabet by Kieta Takahashi and Adam Saltsman
  • Tie Simulator by Ludipe
  • Fake Computer, Real Violence by Matteo Loglio
  • Trackball Driver Installation Disk by Jonny Hopkins
  • how to be born by Danny Hawk
  • Watch Me Dance by Hao Lio
